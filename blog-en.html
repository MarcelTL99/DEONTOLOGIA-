How to Improve Your Legal Translation Skills

Published on: December 9, 2024

Legal translation is not just a linguistic exercise but also a process that requires in-depth knowledge of legal systems and specialized terminology. Here are some tips to enhance your skills as a legal translator.

Legal terminology varies depending on the area of law. For instance, civil procedural law uses different terminology than criminal law. Therefore, it is essential to have adequate knowledge of the relevant legal field.

Additionally, when translating between languages, it is crucial to understand the laws and legal systems of each country. Some laws apply in one country but may be completely unknown in another.

The Importance of Confidentiality in Legal Translation

Published on: December 2, 2024

Confidentiality is one of the most critical aspects of legal translation. Translators have access to sensitive and confidential information, so it is imperative to handle this information with the utmost care.

There are various ways to ensure confidentiality in translation work, such as using confidentiality agreements with clients and securely storing documents. Additionally, encryption technologies can help protect information in the digital realm.

It's also important to consider the use of machine translation tools. Systems like DeepL or Pons learn from inserted translations, which can violate data protection laws. As a result, sworn translators cannot use certain tools.

Is Knowing a Language the Same as Knowing How to Translate?

Published on: November 25, 2024

Speaking a language does not equate to knowing how to translate. Translation requires not only linguistic knowledge but also the ability to interpret contexts, cultural nuances, and adapt the message to be clear and accurate in the target language.

A professional translator faces challenges such as technical, legal terms, or idiomatic expressions that have no exact equivalents. Their job is to convey the original meaning and intention, beyond literal words.

Thus, translation is a specialized skill that combines linguistic mastery, research, and experience. Knowing a language is not enough; it is essential to know how to use it appropriately in each context.
